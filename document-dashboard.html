            Advanced Parameters
            

              Fine-grained WFG2 slider controls. For most training, use the Regenerate/Good flow above instead.
            


          
          

            Section 1 — Core Edge Sensitivity
            

              

                
                
                
Low = only strong color differences create edges. High = moderate differences produce edges.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = shadows/brightness gradients may generate edges. High = brightness-only gradients heavily suppressed.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = loose boundaries. High = edges snap tightly to color transitions.

              

            

          


          
          

            Section 2 — Region Formation
            

              

                
                
                
Low = gradients may split regions. High = internal shading merges into one region.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = small regions easily merge. High = small high-contrast objects remain distinct.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = many small noisy regions survive. High = only significant regions remain.

              

            

          


          
          

            Section 3 — Structural Reinforcement
            

              

                
                
                
Low = color evidence dominates. High = continuous edges get extra weight.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = open boundaries allowed. High = closed shapes strongly preferred.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = preserve raw edges. High = smooth boundaries.

              

            

          


          
          

            Section 4 — Color-First Partition
            

              

                
                
                
Low = strict color matching (more regions). High = permissive (fewer, larger regions).

              

              

                
                
                
Low = strict boundaries. High = repair noisy boundary gaps aggressively.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = narrow tolerance search. High = wide local refinement at boundaries.

              

              

                
                
                
Low = favor small high-contrast regions. High = favor large uniform regions.
